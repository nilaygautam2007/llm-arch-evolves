What does autoregressive factorization decompose?

Show answer

A sequence probability into next-token conditionals given prior tokens.

part-1 · ch-01

Why use cross-entropy for language modeling?

Show answer

It penalizes probability assigned away from observed tokens and is optimized by maximum likelihood.

part-1 · ch-01

What is the central n-gram trade-off?

Show answer

A longer fixed history captures more local context but makes exact counts far sparser.

part-1 · ch-02

What does smoothing accomplish?

Show answer

It reserves probability for rare or unseen events rather than assigning them zero probability.

part-1 · ch-02

How did a neural probabilistic LM address count sparsity?

Show answer

Embeddings let similar contexts share statistical strength.

part-1 · ch-03

What limit remains in a fixed-window neural LM?

Show answer

It cannot condition on information outside its chosen window.

part-1 · ch-03

Why are static word embeddings limited?

Show answer

One vector cannot represent a word's context-dependent senses.

part-1 · ch-04

How do subword tokenizers help?

Show answer

They compose rare words from smaller units and reduce out-of-vocabulary failures.

part-1 · ch-04

Why do vanilla RNNs have unstable long-range gradients?

Show answer

Backpropagation through time multiplies many Jacobians, which can shrink or grow exponentially.

part-2 · ch-05

What compute constraint does recurrence impose?

Show answer

Each state depends on the previous state, so positions cannot be fully processed in parallel.

part-2 · ch-05

What is the LSTM's key gradient-path idea?

Show answer

A gated, nearly additive cell-state path can preserve information and gradients.

part-2 · ch-06

How does a GRU differ conceptually from an LSTM?

Show answer

It consolidates gating and state machinery into a simpler recurrent unit.

part-2 · ch-06

What bottleneck did basic encoder-decoder models expose?

Show answer

A whole source sequence had to fit into one fixed-size encoder vector.

part-2 · ch-07

What mismatch does teacher forcing create?

Show answer

Training conditions on true prior tokens while generation conditions on model predictions.

part-2 · ch-07

What do attention weights do?

Show answer

They route each query to a weighted combination of relevant value vectors.

part-2 · ch-08

Why did attention help sequence-to-sequence learning?

Show answer

It creates short, differentiable paths from a decoder step to selected encoder states.

part-2 · ch-08

Why is positional information needed in self-attention?

Show answer

Attention alone is permutation-equivariant and does not know token order.

part-2 · ch-09

What does multi-head attention buy?

Show answer

Separate learned projections can attend to different relations or representation subspaces.

part-2 · ch-09

Why is decoder-only training naturally suited to generation?

Show answer

The causal objective directly trains the next-token prediction used at decode time.

part-3 · ch-10

What does the KV cache avoid?

Show answer

Recomputing keys and values for all earlier tokens on every generated token.

part-3 · ch-10

How do MLM and causal LM differ in their inference contract?

Show answer

MLM uses bidirectional masked prediction; causal LM uses left-to-right next-token prediction.

part-3 · ch-11

Why does data mixture matter?

Show answer

The training distribution shapes which patterns, languages, and capabilities receive learning signal.

part-3 · ch-11

What did Chinchilla-style analysis change?

Show answer

It emphasized balancing model size and training tokens under a compute budget.

part-3 · ch-12

Why not treat tokens-per-parameter as a universal constant?

Show answer

It is a fitted, objective- and setup-dependent rule of thumb.

part-3 · ch-12

What serving cost does GQA reduce?

Show answer

KV-cache memory and bandwidth by sharing K/V heads among query-head groups.

part-4 · ch-13

What does RoPE encode?

Show answer

Relative-position structure in query-key dot products through rotations.

part-4 · ch-13

What is MoE's core capacity trade-off?

Show answer

It raises total parameters with sparse activation but adds routing and communication complexity.

part-4 · ch-14

Why is load balancing needed in MoE?

Show answer

Overused experts become bottlenecks while underused experts waste capacity.

part-4 · ch-14

What is FlashAttention's key idea?

Show answer

Compute exact attention in tiles with online softmax instead of storing the full score matrix.

part-4 · ch-15

How do training attention cost and decode KV growth differ?

Show answer

Full attention has quadratic token interactions; cached history grows linearly with generated length.

part-4 · ch-15

What memory trade-off do SSMs make?

Show answer

They retain a bounded recurrent state rather than an explicit growing token KV cache.

part-4 · ch-16

What is Mamba's selectivity?

Show answer

Its state dynamics depend on the input, allowing content-dependent state updates.

part-4 · ch-16

What does RAG externalize?

Show answer

Mutable facts and evidence into a retrievable non-parametric index.

part-5 · ch-17

Why can RAG still fail?

Show answer

Retrieval, chunking, reranking, context placement, and generation can each introduce errors.

part-5 · ch-17

What is a multimodal projector?

Show answer

A module mapping modality-encoder features into representations an LLM can consume.

part-5 · ch-18

Why is video expensive for multimodal models?

Show answer

Time adds many visual token groups, multiplying context and compute costs.

part-5 · ch-18

What makes test-time compute a system technique?

Show answer

It adds sampling, search, traces, and verification around a base forward pass.

part-5 · ch-19

How do PRMs and ORMs differ?

Show answer

PRMs score intermediate reasoning steps; ORMs score final outcomes.

part-5 · ch-19

What should choose an architecture first?

Show answer

The binding constraint—such as capacity, memory, updateability, or latency.

part-6 · ch-20

What is the broad dense Transformer advantage?

Show answer

Flexible content-addressed interaction and highly parallel training.

part-6 · ch-20

Name one unresolved architecture tension.

Show answer

Examples: memory vs compute, sparsity vs stability, or generalization vs memorization.

part-6 · ch-21

Why should open tensions not be treated as solved?

Show answer

Their best trade-off depends on workload, data, hardware, and evaluation.

part-6 · ch-21

What is a productive architecture-research starting point?

Show answer

Measure a concrete binding constraint, state the desired relaxation, and name the new trade-off.

part-6 · ch-22

Why require a failure analysis?

Show answer

It tests whether a gain comes from the proposed mechanism rather than a confound.

part-6 · ch-22

What durable design lens closes the study?

Show answer

Successful architectures reallocate constraints rather than eliminate them.

part-6 · ch-23

What distinguishes a system innovation from an architecture innovation?

Show answer

A system changes retrieval, search, or serving around a model; architecture changes its computation graph.
