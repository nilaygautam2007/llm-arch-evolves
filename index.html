A constraint-first workflow

1. Locate the bottleneck

Is the limiting resource data, gradient path, sequential time, capacity, memory, bandwidth, or factual freshness?

2. Name the reallocation

Attention routes information; MoE routes capacity; RAG routes queries to external evidence.

3. Test the new cost

Ask about routing instability, quadratic interactions, cache growth, communication, or retrieval failure.

View timeline as an accessible list
  1. Statistical n-grams: short histories avoid unbounded tables but are sparse.
  2. Embeddings and recurrent state: share patterns and extend history, but complicate optimization.
  3. Attention and Transformers: make routing and parallel mixing explicit, but grow interaction/memory cost.
  4. MoE, long-context, and SSMs: reallocate capacity and sequence memory under deployment constraints.
  5. RAG, multimodality, and reasoning: make the system around the model part of the design.

Concept map: family relationships, not dates

  • N-grams → embeddings → recurrent models.
  • Recurrent models + attention → Transformer.
    • Transformer → decoder-only models → RAG and reasoning systems.
    • Transformer → MoE and long-context methods.
  • Recurrent ideas inspire state-space models.
